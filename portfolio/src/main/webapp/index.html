About Me

My full name is Thomas Joaquin Kaufman-Gomez, but I usually just go by Tommy Gomez. I'm a rising junior at Columbia University in New York City, and I'm studying computer science. Born and raised in Sunnyvale, California, I've lived on both coasts and can confidently confirm that the West Coast is superior, although the East Coast is not to be undervalued (in fact, I would argue that both coasts are equal if it weren't for the East's weather).

For as long as I can remember, I've developed strong, unpredictable, and often unusual passions (check out the Interests tab for specifics). These passions are sort of like intellectual rabbit holes, because I dedicate most of my time to learning as much about them as I can. This made declaring a major difficult, as I was stuck in multiple rabbit holes and had to focus on one. I found that computer science was like the ultimate rabbit hole; it's a subject so versatile that it has applications in every field imaginable. To me, computer science is the culmination of my passions, because it gives me the limitless ability to build, model, and analyze anything that interests me. There's nothing I'd rather do for the rest of my life.

Astronomy

Early childhood

The first thing I remember being interested in is astronomy. Before I could read, I would look at pictures of planets, stars, and galaxies, amazed by their shiny rings, bright colors, and swirling patterns. Once I was a little older, the facts I learned about astronomy shocked me. I couldn't believe that a day on Venus is longer than a year (that one still baffles me), or that the hottest stars burn blue. What I found most appealing about astronomy, however, was how little we know. To this day, I wonder about how the universe began, what storms are like on other planets, what happens when galaxies collide, and of course whether or not aliens exist (I think they do ðŸ‘½), among many other things. There's so much to learn about the universe, which is why this interest has never quite left me.
